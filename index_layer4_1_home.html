What is TRIZEL?

TRIZEL is a structured system for documenting and presenting AI governance research. It operates under a strict multi-layer architecture where each layer has a specific role and clearly defined boundaries.

TRIZEL is NOT:

  • An operational AI system
  • A live monitoring or analytics platform
  • A research publication venue
  • A decision-making authority
  • A predictive or inference engine
  • An interactive application with system behavior

TRIZEL provides institutional documentation and presentation of governance research conducted under strict layer separation principles.

Layer Structure

The TRIZEL system operates under a mandatory layer separation model. Each layer has specific responsibilities and strict boundaries:

Layer-0 Governance / Authority

Repository: trizel-core

Establishes policies, standards, and governance framework. This is the authoritative source for all TRIZEL system decisions and constraints.

Authority: Final decision-making power

Layer-1 Internal Research & Analysis

Repositories: trizel-lab (public); trizel-AI, trizel-monitor (internal)

Conducts research, analysis, and validation under Layer-0 governance. Internal research operations and technical execution.

Authority: Scientific and technical execution

Layer-2 Presentation / Display

Repository: trizel-site (this site)

Public-facing presentation and orientation. Structural descriptions and navigation aids only. No scientific content or authority.

Authority: None — Presentation-only

Layer-2.5 Epistemic Mapping
